Why The Need For Data Driven Agriculture?

Correlation with the Environment

Environment, soil and irrigation factors have direct correlation with agricultural productivity and yield.

Lack of Resources

Lack of necessary tools to identify, analyze and manage variability in environment , soil and irrigation factors by conducting crop production practices at the right place and time and in the right way, for optimum profitability, sustainability farming practices.

Lack of Data

Lack of data to define a decision support system (DSS) for whole farm management with the goal of optimizing returns on inputs while preserving resources.

Solution

An IoT and AI enabled Farm Management Software Platform which collects, analyzes various agricultural input parameters and provide recommendations during the entire agricultural life cycle process with the help of AI models and human consultation using agricultural Scientists, KVKs and FPOs.

Request Demo

  • Software

    Hyper-local current weather, weather forecast, Satellite imagery , Soil and UVI Index for a particular agricultural field.

  • Hardware

    Ground Level Soil, air and Water parameters with the help of Sensors using LPWAN ( Low Powe Wide Area network) Technology.

  • Expert Consultation

    Expert consultation through agricultural scientist, KVKs( Krishi Vigyan Kendra) and FPOs (Farmer producer Organizations).

  • AI Modeling

    AI Models which does predictive analytics and What if scenarios with the help of Data Clustering, Data Classification and Regression analysis.

Benefits of Smart Farming


  • Farmers and landowners who decide to use technology to manage their fields, precision farming seems to bring many benefits, and ultimately increase profit.

  • Improved farming procedures like Crop Growth/Irrigation decisions, Fertilizer Timing and Delivery decisions, Pest and Disease Control monitoring and Field Workability decisions.

  • More Cost-effective farming.

  • Higher yield and more productivity.

  • Better Quality produce.

  • Less Waste of the most precious natural resources.

  • Less Debt accumulation due to less costs of farming.

  • Less strain to the environment and more focus on what the crop actually needs not on what we think they need.

  • Higher Return on Investment.
